# Breathing Exercises: Your Built-In Regulation Tool

## Why Breathing Works

Your breath is the only part of your autonomic nervous system you can consciously control.

When you change your breathing, you directly signal your nervous system:
- Slow breathing = safety
- Long exhales = activate parasympathetic (calm)
- Rhythmic breathing = regulation

This isn't woo. It's biology. Breathing exercises have measurable effects on heart rate variability, cortisol levels, and nervous system state.

## The Basics

### Exhale Longer Than Inhale

The simplest regulation technique: make your exhale longer than your inhale.

**Why:** Exhaling activates the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest). Inhaling activates sympathetic (fight or flight).

**How:**
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Breathe out for 6-8 counts
- Repeat 5-10 times

That's it. Simple and effective.

## Specific Techniques

### Box Breathing (Square Breathing)

Used by Navy SEALs for stress regulation.

**How:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Repeat

**Good for:** Acute stress, anxiety, need to focus, before difficult conversations.

### 4-7-8 Breathing

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil. Particularly good for sleep.

**How:**
- Inhale through nose for 4 counts
- Hold for 7 counts
- Exhale through mouth for 8 counts
- Repeat 4 cycles

**Good for:** Falling asleep, acute anxiety, calming down quickly.

### Physiological Sigh

The fastest way to calm down (research by Andrew Huberman).

**How:**
- Two quick inhales through nose (first fills lungs, second tops them off)
- One long exhale through mouth
- Repeat 1-3 times

**Good for:** Real-time calming in moments of stress. Takes seconds.

### Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing)

Breathing into your belly rather than chest.

**How:**
- Place one hand on chest, one on belly
- Breathe so that belly hand rises, chest hand stays relatively still
- Slow, deep breaths
- Continue for several minutes

**Good for:** General regulation, anxiety, building a calming practice.

### Coherent Breathing

Equal inhales and exhales at about 5-6 breaths per minute.

**How:**
- Inhale for 5-6 counts
- Exhale for 5-6 counts
- Continue for 5-20 minutes

**Good for:** Daily practice, building heart rate variability, long-term nervous system health.

## When to Use What

**Panic or acute anxiety:** Physiological sigh (fastest) or extended exhale
**Before sleep:** 4-7-8 breathing
**Need to focus:** Box breathing
**Daily practice:** Coherent breathing or diaphragmatic breathing
**In the moment stress:** Any of these - the one you remember

## How to Practice

### Start simple
Pick one technique. Practice it when you're calm. Get familiar with it so it's available when you need it.

### Regular practice
A few minutes daily builds capacity. Your nervous system becomes more responsive to breathing cues.

### Use it early
Don't wait until you're in full crisis. Use breathing at first signs of activation.

### Don't force it
If a technique feels uncomfortable or increases anxiety, try a different one. Not every technique works for everyone.

### Be patient
It might not work instantly. Give it a minute or two. Regulation takes time.

## Common Mistakes

**Breathing too fast:** Slow down. Fast deep breathing can cause hyperventilation.

**Chest breathing:** Let the breath go to your belly, not just your chest.

**Forcing it:** Gentle, not aggressive. You're calming, not conquering.

**Giving up too soon:** Give it at least 5-10 breaths before deciding it's not working.

**Only using it in crisis:** Regular practice makes it more effective when you need it.

## When Breathing Isn't Enough

Breathing is a first-line tool. Sometimes you need more:
- Physical movement to discharge energy
- Connection with another person
- Longer time to regulate
- Addressing the underlying cause

Breathing helps in the moment. It doesn't replace deeper work.

## Building a Practice

**Morning:** 2-3 minutes of coherent breathing to start the day regulated.

**Throughout day:** Notice when you're activated. Take 3 conscious breaths.

**Before stressful situations:** Box breathing or physiological sighs.

**Before bed:** 4-7-8 breathing to transition to sleep.

**When triggered:** Whatever technique you remember. Breathing always helps.
